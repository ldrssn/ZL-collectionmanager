title: "Case Study: Information Architecture & Discovery at Scale"
category: "UX Strategy"
As users' personal collections grew into the hundreds, traditional linear search and basic category lists became insufficient. The project required a complete overhaul of its discovery flows to support rapid retrieval and complex data exploration.

## Strategic Objectives
- **Cognitive Load Reduction**: Move from simple lists to an intuitive, multi-dimensional discovery engine.
- **Advanced Retrieval**: Enable users to explore complex intersections of their data through concurrent constraints.
- **Mobile-First Discovery**: Ensure high-powered search capabilities remain accessible and intuitive on mobile devices.

## Key Outcomes
- **Concurrent Filter Engine**: Re-engineered the search architecture to support multi-select categorical discovery, allowing for deep exploration of collection "intersections" in real-time.
- **Visual State Management**: Developed a "Filter Pill" navigation system that provides immediate feedback on active constraints, allowing for granular dismissal and preventing "search-blindness."
- **UX Optimization**: Implemented horizontally-scrolling navigation patterns for mobile to maximize screen real estate, ensuring the discovery experience remains fluid regardless of the complexity of the query.
